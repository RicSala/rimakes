## On-brand (gold standard)

<!-- ✏️ REPLACE — 2-3 real pieces. For each: the channel, the piece (verbatim), and *why*

EXAMPLE (Vela):

### Example 1 — LinkedIn post
> Your standup is a meeting to recap work nobody read.
>
> So we tried something: write the update once, everyone reads it on their own time. Three
> weeks in, we've cut 4 hours of meetings a week and decisions are actually findable.
>
> Here's the template we use →

**Why it works:** leads with pillar #1 (ship more by meeting less), "clear over clever",
"calm not hypey" (no emojis, no exclamation), and a concrete number instead of an adjective.

### Example 2 — Release note
> **Scheduled updates are here.** You can now write Friday's recap on Thursday and have it
> post itself. One less thing to remember on a Friday afternoon.

**Why it works:** benefit-first, factual, dry humour, no hype — matches the release-notes tone.

### Example 3 — Cold email opener
> Hi Maya — saw your team just doubled headcount. More people usually means more status
> meetings. We built Vela so it doesn't have to. Worth five minutes?

**Why it works:** speaks to the primary persona's real pain, peer-to-peer, brief, candid.
